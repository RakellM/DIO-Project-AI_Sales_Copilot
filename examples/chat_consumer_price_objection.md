# Example Chat: Customer Price Objection

## Scenario
Customer says meals are too expensive compared to Tim Hortons.

## Sales Rep Query to AI Copilot
"Customer says '$14 for lunch? I can get Tim Hortons for \$6.' Help me respond."

## AI Copilot Output

## SUGGESTED RESPONSE

"I hear you - Tim Hortons is cheaper. But let me ask: what would you normally spend on lunch downtown? Most of our customers were spending \$18-22 on UberEats or \$15 at places like Pure Kitchen. With TasteCraft, you get Ontario-raised beef, never-frozen ingredients, and meals tailored to your health goals - whether that's Protein Forward for athletes or GLP-1 friendly for gentle eating. Want to try your first bowl with 25% off? Code CRAFT25 works today."

## INTERNAL NOTES (Rep Only)

- Customer persona: Likely Persona 5 (Skeptical First-Timer) or Persona 1 (Professional)
- Key objection: Price anchoring (comparing to fast food, not comparable quality)
- Recommended next action: Offer first order discount, don't push subscription
- Watch out for: If they mention Tim Hortons specifically, they may be very budget-conscious - consider mentioning our \$11.95 Smoky Black Bean bowl as entry point

## FOLLOW-UP QUESTION TO ASK CUSTOMER

"What's your typical lunch budget downtown? I can show you how TasteCraft compares to what you're already spending."

## Outcome
Customer replies: "Usually \$15-18 at Salad Days. Okay, I'll try the 25% off. Send me the code."

Rep sends code. Customer converts to first order (5 meals).
